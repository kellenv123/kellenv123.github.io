The Nēnē, or Hawaiian Goose, is the rarest goose in the world! It inhabits the main Hawaiian islands and can be found in varied terrain, from manicured golf courses and parks up to high-elevation, barren lava fields. The Nēnē are an example of one of the most successful conservation efforts, having been brought back from the brink of extinction. In 1952 there were only 30 wild geese and today there are nearly 4,000 indivduals.
